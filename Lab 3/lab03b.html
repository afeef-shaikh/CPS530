Discover Mozambique

Geography

Mozambique is located in Southeast Africa, bordered by the Indian Ocean to the east, Tanzania to the north, Malawi and Zambia to the northwest, Zimbabwe to the west, and Eswatini and South Africa to the southwest. The country is known for its stunning coastline, rich biodiversity, and beautiful natural landscapes, which include mountains, plains, rivers, and lakes.

History

Mozambique has a long and complex history that dates back to ancient times. It was colonized by the Portuguese in the early 16th century and gained independence in 1975 after a prolonged struggle for liberation. Since then, Mozambique has faced challenges including civil war, but it has made significant progress in recent decades towards peace and development.

Culture

The culture of Mozambique is vibrant and diverse, influenced by African, Portuguese, and Arab traditions. The country is famous for its music and dance forms, especially the marimba and timbila instruments, as well as traditional dances such as the Makonde. Mozambique is also home to rich culinary traditions, featuring dishes made with cassava, maize, and fresh seafood.
